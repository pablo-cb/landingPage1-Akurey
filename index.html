Staff Augmentation

Expert Tips for Success

Updated: 03 may 2023
Reading Time: 6 mins

Businesses often require additional skill sets and bandwidth in programming, user interface design, or software quality assurance to achieve their goals. Staff augmentation can be a flexible and cost-effective solution for businesses of all sizes. However, there are situations in which it becomes an urgent need:

  • Meeting tight deadlines: When companies need to complete a project quickly, temporary staffing can increase bandwidth and ensure on-time delivery.
 
  • Addressing unexpected demand: When companies face sudden spikes in demand, temporary staffing can help handle increased workloads while maintaining quality.
 
  • Responding to crisis situations: When companies encounter crisis situations, temporary staffing with specialized expertise can help address the issue quickly and effectively.

To help businesses find the right partner for their staffing needs, we have put together three key considerations:

Management

Proper Workload Management

Ensure that the staff augmentation provider has techniques in place to manage workloads and prevent burnout. Overloading consultants can lead to decreased quality of work and increased healthcare spending.

A Harvard Business Review survey shows that employee burnout costs $125-190 billion in US healthcare cost annually.
Define Roles and Responsibilities

Ensure their consultants have expertise in industry and security standards, as well as software programming languages, not only selecting the consultants you require right now, but also evaluating the available expertise from your provider. This will help them integrate these standards into their work seamlessly, resulting in higher quality and performance.

According to a McKinsey study, a clear definition of roles and responsibilities improves team performance by 20%.

Expertise

Adherence to Industry Standards and Best Practices

Evaluate the provider's documented software development procedures and coding standards to ensure quality work that meets industry high standards such as:

Scrum methodology
Ticket estimation techniques
CI/CD automation
Gitflow protocols
UX requirements
QA practices
Availability of Experienced Consultants

Evaluate whether the provider has a network of experienced consultants available to resolve complex issues quickly and efficiently.

Security Considerations

Evaluate how the provider addresses security concerns to protect your business's sensitive information and ensure compliance with relevant regulations.

Culture &

Time Zone

Consider Cultural Fit

Choose a staff augmentation partner with compatible work culture and values to ensure seamless integration of augmented team members and alignment with project goals, keeping them motivated and invested.

Distance between Teams

Consider the distance between your business and the provider's team. A significant time zone difference can lead to delays in communication and project progress.

It is essential to evaluate whether the provider's team is located in a similar time zone to yours.

In conclusion, selecting the right service provider is crucial to the success of your staff augmentation project. Considering these key factors, you can select a provider that aligns with your business's needs and goals.
